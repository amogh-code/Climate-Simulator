This graph shows how the atmospheric temperature changes based on wind, greenhouse gases, and energy absorption.

Ocean temperatures rise and fall depending on heat absorption from both the atmosphere and land surfaces.

Land temperature is influenced by both ocean and atmosphere temperatures and directly affects sea ice melt rates.

This graph visualizes the changes in sea ice volume over time, showing the impact of rising ocean temperatures.

Parameter Descriptions

A_OCEAN: Ocean heat absorption coefficient (0.1-0.5)

A_LAND: Land heat absorption coefficient (0.1-0.5)

A_ICE: Sea ice heat absorption coefficient (0.05-0.2)

ICE_MELT_RATE: Rate of ice melting (0.01-0.1)

TIME_STEPS: Number of years for simulation (50-200)

Wind Magnitude: Wind speed affecting climate (1-10 m/s)

Greenhouse Gases: Level of greenhouse gases normalized (0.5-2)
